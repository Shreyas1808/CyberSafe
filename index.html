Common Cyber Threats

Learn about the most prevalent cyber threats and how to protect yourself

What is Phishing? +

Phishing is a scam where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations (like your bank or email provider) via email, text, or phone calls to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords and credit card numbers. Tip: Never click on suspicious links and always check the sender's email address.

What is Malware? +

Malware (Malicious Software) is software designed to harm or gain unauthorized access to a computer system. This includes viruses, ransomware, and spyware. It often spreads through malicious downloads or email attachments. Tip: Use a reputable antivirus and avoid downloading software from untrusted sources.

How can I create a strong password? +

Use a long passphrase (a sequence of random words) instead of a single complex word. For example, Blue-Coffee-Mug-Bounces-22! is much stronger than P@ssw0rd. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible for an extra layer of security.
